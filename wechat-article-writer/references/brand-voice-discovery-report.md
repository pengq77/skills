## 2. Voice Attributes

### Primary Tone: Warm, Conversational, Understated Enthusiasm

The writing feels like chatting with a knowledgeable friend — personal, relaxed, and genuine. There's clear passion for the subjects (cycling, tech) but it's never preachy or over-the-top. The author lets experiences speak for themselves rather than hyping them up.

**Key descriptors (ranked by consistency across articles)**:

| Attribute | Strength | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Conversational / 口语化 | Very Strong | First-person narrative throughout, casual sentence structures |
| Authentic / 真实 | Very Strong | Personal anecdotes, admits limitations and imperfections |
| Practical / 实用 | Strong | Embeds useful info (routes, specs, transit tips) within personal stories |

## 3. Language Style

### 3.1 Bilingual Code-Switching
The most distinctive language feature. English terms are woven naturally into Chinese text — not translated, not explained, just used as-is. This reflects the bilingual lifestyle of the target audience.

**Examples**: "drive through 咖啡店休息一下", "Sunrise 日出公路", "Sound Transit 的轻轨", "mini PC", "Claude Code", "Discord"

**Pattern**: English used for proper nouns (place names, product names, tech terms), Chinese used for descriptions, opinions, and emotions.

### 3.2 Sentence Structure
- **Short paragraphs**: Typically 1-3 sentences per paragraph
- **Direct and concise**: No flowery literary language, no excessive adjectives
- **Conversational connectors**: 不过、其实、另外、最后 (casual transition words)
- **Parenthetical asides**: Brief personal commentary embedded in descriptions

### 3.3 Punctuation & Markers
- Uses `:)` western emoticons (not Chinese/emoji style)
- Occasional `lol` mixed into Chinese text
- Uses `...` for trailing thoughts
- Hashtags on some articles (#竞速瓜车体验 #自行车与自然风光 #单车爱好者的日常)
- Ends articles with disclaimer: "作者提示 个人观点，仅供参考"

## 4. Content Structure Patterns

### Typical Article Architecture:

1. **Hook / Context**: Brief setup — why this topic, what prompted the article (often data-driven: "根据Strava上的骑行记录...")
2. **Personal narrative**: First-person experience, chronological or thematic
3. **Practical information**: Specific details (distances, specs, routes, setup steps) embedded naturally in the story
4. **Photo-heavy sections**: Large photos with short 1-2 sentence captions — visuals carry significant weight
5. **Reflection / Wrap-up**: Short personal takeaway, often with light humor or forward-looking sentiment

### Content Mix by Topic:
- **Cycling** (3/5 articles): Routes, gear, commuting — the core identity
- **Tech/DIY** (1/5 articles): Hands-on setup guides with personal commentary
- **Lifestyle/Cross-cultural** (1/5 articles): China-US comparisons through cycling lens
- **Overlap**: Most articles blend 2+ topics (cycling + tech, cycling + travel, cycling + commuting)

## 8. Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- Write in first person, as if telling a friend about your experience
- Mix English and Chinese naturally (don't force-translate proper nouns)
- Lead with personal experience, embed practical info within the story
- Use lots of high-quality photos with short captions
- Include specific data points (distances, times, specs) to ground the narrative
- End with a light, humble reflection
- Keep paragraphs short (mobile-friendly)

### DON'T:
- Use formal/literary Chinese or marketing-speak
- Over-explain English terms to the audience (they're bilingual)
- Write purely informational/guide-style content without personal narrative
- Use excessive emojis or internet slang (tone is mature-casual, not youth-casual)
- Make grand claims or be preachy about cycling/sustainability
- Neglect visuals — the articles are as much photography as writing

## 10. Summary

The 骑思光年 brand voice is that of a **thoughtful, bilingual cycling enthusiast sharing authentic Pacific Northwest experiences with a Chinese-speaking audience**. The tone is warm and conversational, the content is practical and photo-rich, and the writing naturally bridges two cultures without making that the point. The most distinctive features are the seamless Chinese-English code-switching and the understated, self-deprecating humor that runs through even technical content.
