# 骑思光年 Brand Voice Guidelines

> These guidelines define the voice, tone, and style for the WeChat public account 骑思光年 (SummerSnow). Use them when creating or reviewing any content for this brand.

---

## Voice Identity

**One-line summary**: A bilingual cycling enthusiast sharing authentic Pacific Northwest life with warmth, practical detail, and quiet humor.

**Voice archetype**: The knowledgeable friend — someone who's done the thing, enjoyed it, and wants to tell you about it over coffee. Not a guru, not an influencer, just a person with good taste and genuine experience.

---

## Core Voice Attributes

### 1. Conversational (口语化)
Write as you speak. First person. Short paragraphs. No academic or literary Chinese. The reader should feel like they're reading a friend's message, not an article.

**Do**: "今年最大的投入之一，给夫人装了一辆新的瓜车，不过目前使用率有点低。"
**Don't**: "本年度最为重要的器材投资之一为为配偶组装的全新砾石公路车。"

### 2. Authentic (真实)
Share real experiences including imperfections, limitations, and honest opinions. Don't idealize or over-polish. Admit when something didn't work out or when you're still figuring it out.

**Do**: "不过自从当了Claude Code，我的手工debug能力退化严重"
**Don't**: "通过使用先进AI工具，我的开发效率实现了质的飞跃"

### 3. Practically Informative (实用)
Embed useful details (distances, specs, routes, steps, costs) naturally within the personal narrative. Don't write pure how-to guides — let the information emerge from the experience.

**Do**: "从偶尔的长途骑行搭轻轨返程偷个懒，到每天的上下班。"
**Don't**: "步骤一：查看轻轨时刻表。步骤二：将自行车带上轻轨。"

### 4. Quietly Enthusiastic (内敛的热情)
Show passion through what you choose to write about and the details you notice — not through exclamation marks or superlatives. Understatement is the brand's humor style.

**Do**: "哪怕没事干的时候，就在里面静静看会自己的自行车们也是一种享受:)"
**Don't**: "太爱我的自行车了！！！每天看着它们超级开心！！！"

### 5. Cross-Cultural (跨文化)
Naturally reference both Chinese and American contexts. Compare without judging. Let the reader draw their own conclusions from the observations.

**Do**: "比如说，我玩无人机不久，要是换个中国调频的话……"
**Don't**: "中国的无人机政策比美国好/差多了。"

## Language Rules

### Chinese-English Code-Switching
This is the most distinctive feature of the brand voice. Follow these rules:

- **Proper nouns stay in English**: Strava, Sound Transit, Sunrise, Discord, Claude Code, Marymoor
- **Technical terms can stay in English**: mini PC, drive through, API key, npm
- **Place names**: Use English for US locations (Arlington, Bend, Overlake), Chinese for Chinese locations (戒台寺, 滇池)
- **Never force-translate** well-known English terms — the audience is bilingual
- **Descriptions, opinions, and emotions** should be in Chinese
- **Don't explain** English terms parenthetically unless truly obscure

### Sentence & Paragraph Style
- **Paragraph length**: 1-3 sentences. Rarely more than 4.
- **No bullet points** in articles (use flowing prose)
- **Section headers**: Bold, short, descriptive (e.g., "骑行装备", "华盛顿DC", "骑行+AI")
- **Transitions**: Use casual connectors (不过、其实、另外、最后、说回正题)

### Punctuation & Emoji
- Use `：）` or `:)` sparingly for light humor — western emoticon style
- Occasional `lol` in Chinese context is acceptable
- Use `...` for trailing/reflective thoughts
- **No emoji** (not the brand style)
- **No excessive exclamation marks**

### Hashtags (when used)
- 3-4 per article maximum
- Chinese hashtags, descriptive not clever: #竞速瓜车体验 #自行车与自然风光 #单车爱好者的日常

## Content Structure Template

### Standard Article Format:

1. **Opening hook** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Context or data point that sets up the topic
   - Why you're writing about this now

2. **Main body** (photo-driven sections)
   - Organized by sub-topic, location, or chronology
   - Each section: bold header → 1-3 paragraphs of narrative → photos with captions
   - Practical info woven into the personal story

3. **Reflection / Closing** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Brief personal takeaway
   - Often light humor or forward-looking sentiment
   - Optional: "作者提示 个人观点，仅供参考"

### Photo Guidelines:
- Photos carry equal weight to text — they're not illustrations, they're content
- Use large, high-quality images (scenic, equipment, infrastructure)
- Short captions (one sentence) under photos
- Strava maps/screenshots for route documentation
- AI-generated images acceptable for headers/creative content

## Tone Adjustments by Context

| Context | Tone shift |
|---------|------------|
| Annual review articles | Slightly more reflective, data-driven, comprehensive |
| Gear/equipment | More technical detail, but still personal ("我发现...") |
| Travel/adventure | More descriptive, photo-forward, light storytelling |
| Tech/DIY | Step-by-step narrated in first person, self-deprecating about difficulties |
| Short social posts | Even more casual, can use lol and :), shorter |

## Things to Avoid

- **Marketing language**: No "颠覆性", "极致体验", "强烈推荐"
- **Formal/literary tone**: No 之乎者也 or academic phrasing
- **Over-explaining**: Don't condescend; assume the reader is smart and bilingual
- **Preachiness**: Don't lecture about cycling benefits, sustainability, etc.
- **Excessive emoji or internet slang**: The tone is mature-casual, not youth-casual
- **Pure listicles or guides**: Always anchor in personal experience
- **Negativity or complaints**: Observations yes, rants no
- **Clickbait titles**: Titles should be descriptive and genuine

## Title Style

Titles should be:
- Descriptive and specific, not clickbaity
- Can include English terms naturally
- Often state the core topic directly
- Can be a personal statement or observation

**Good examples from existing content**:
- "2025 骑行记录，继续在路上"
- "雨季每日最佳通勤方式: 轻轨+自行车"
- "如果只能有一辆自行车的话，那一定是一辆竞速瓜车"
- "总算把OpenClaw 搭起来了，终于知道大家为啥这么激动了"
